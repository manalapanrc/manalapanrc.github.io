The Constitution of The Manalapan Run Club

The governing principles of The Manalapan Run Club are not suggestions. They are the non-negotiable standards to which every member commits upon joining.

Preamble

We the Runners

We the Runners of the Manalapan Run Club, in Order to form a more perfect Union of athletes, establish Community among our members, insure domestic Tranquility on our runs, provide for the common Running, promote the general Fitness, and secure the Blessings of Health to ourselves and our Posterity, do ordain and establish this Constitution for the Manalapan Run Club.

📜 Image — Club Charter / Founding Ceremony
I

The Legislative Branch — The Council of Elders

Section 1: All legislative Powers herein granted shall be vested in a Council of Elders of the Manalapan Run Club, which shall consist of the Founding Members and those inducted by unanimous vote.

Section 2: The Council shall have Power to establish training schedules, determine routes, set membership standards, and create traditions that honor the spirit of distance running.

Section 3: No member may serve on the Council who has not completed at least one half-marathon or demonstrated extraordinary commitment to the Club's mission.

II

The Executive Branch — The Captain

Section 1: The executive Power shall be vested in a Captain of the Manalapan Run Club, elected annually by the membership.

Section 2: The Captain shall be responsible for organizing group runs, maintaining communication among members, representing the Club in community events, and upholding the principles set forth in this Constitution.

Section 3: The Captain may be removed from office by a two-thirds vote of the membership for conduct unbecoming of a runner or failure to fulfill their duties.

III

The Judicial Branch — The Court of Miles

Section 1: The judicial Power shall be vested in a Court of Miles, consisting of three senior members appointed by the Council of Elders.

Section 2: The Court shall have jurisdiction over disputes between members, allegations of constitutional violations, and questions regarding the interpretation of Club principles.

Section 3: All decisions of the Court shall be made by majority vote and are final, though the full membership may override a decision by three-fourths vote.

IV

Relations Among Members

Section 1: Full Faith and Credit shall be given in the Club to the running achievements and personal records of every member, regardless of where or when they were accomplished.

Section 2: The members of this Club shall be entitled to all Privileges and Immunities of Runners, including the right to run at their own pace, the right to rest when needed, and the right to be supported by fellow members.

Section 3: New members may be admitted by majority vote of existing members, provided they demonstrate commitment to the Club's values and the sport of distance running.

V

The Amendment Process

The Council of Elders, whenever two-thirds of them shall deem it necessary, shall propose Amendments to this Constitution, or, on the Application of two-thirds of the membership, shall call a meeting for proposing Amendments, which shall be valid when ratified by three-fourths of the members.

VI

Supremacy of the Run

Section 1: This Constitution, and the traditions of the Manalapan Run Club which shall be made in pursuance thereof, shall be the supreme Law of the Club.

Section 2: All members shall be bound by oath or affirmation to support this Constitution and to uphold the values of discipline, community, and athletic excellence.

Section 3: No religious test, political affiliation, or athletic background shall ever be required as a qualification to membership in the Manalapan Run Club — only a genuine love of running and commitment to self-improvement.

VII

Ratification

The Ratification of the Founding Members shall be sufficient for the Establishment of this Constitution for the Manalapan Run Club. Done in Convention by the Unanimous Consent of the Founding Members present, this Year of our Running, Two Thousand and Twenty-Five.

The Bill of Rights

Amendments to the Constitution

Ratified by the membership to protect the fundamental freedoms and rights of all runners within the Club.

I

Freedom of Pace

The Club shall make no rule respecting the establishment of a minimum pace, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of running style, or the right of members to assemble for runs of any distance.

II

Right to Bear Hydration

A well-hydrated Runner, being necessary to the completion of long distances, the right of the members to keep and bear water bottles, shall not be infringed.

III

Protection from Excessive Training

No member shall be compelled to run beyond their current ability, nor shall any member be pressured to race when injured or recovering.

IV

Right to Privacy in Training

The right of members to be secure in their personal training plans, GPS data, and performance metrics shall not be violated, and no member shall be required to share such information without consent.

V

Due Process in Disciplinary Matters

No member shall be expelled or suspended without due process; nor shall any member be subjected to unreasonable criticism for a bad race, nor be deprived of membership without just cause.

VI

Right to a Fair Hearing

In all disputes within the Club, the accused member shall enjoy the right to a speedy and public hearing by an impartial panel of fellow runners.

VII

Right to Rest Days

The right to recovery shall be preserved, and no member shall be shamed for taking necessary rest days or reducing mileage when prudent.

VIII

Protection from Cruel and Unusual Workouts

Excessive track repeats shall not be required, nor cruel and unusual hill workouts inflicted, nor unreasonable weekly mileage imposed.

IX

Rights Retained by the Runners

The enumeration in this Constitution of certain rights shall not be construed to deny or disparage others retained by the members, including the right to run alone, to explore new routes, and to pursue personal running goals.

X

Powers Reserved to the Members

The powers not delegated to the Council of Elders by this Constitution, nor prohibited by it to the members, are reserved to the members respectively, or to the running community at large.

"We hold these truths to be self-evident, that all runners are created equal, that they are endowed by their training with certain unalienable Rights, that among these are Miles, Liberty and the pursuit of PRs."

— The Founding Members, 2025
