The Club Constitution, First Treatise

The founding document establishing the governance structure and principles of The Manalapan Run Club.

The Manalapan Run Club (henceforth known as the 'Club') finds itself in need of a set of rules in order to guide it on all Its future matters. The Club has outlined them below. All stipulations are presented in the manner of 'Articles.'

Article of the Sextet

The Club will contain in it a rigid structure. The divine leaders, now known as the Sextet (nominally the 'Elders') are: Vasu Govardhanen, Nicholas Borzomati, Joshua Perle, Matthew Butch, Michael Black, and Clay Stevens. All matters will be decided by the Sextet, and of course they shall reflect the views of the Clubmen proper. All ties will be broken by the President, who, for the following calendar year (the year twenty-six past our second millennium), is ordained to be Govardhanen.

Article of the Serpents

It was revealed to the Sextet at a time of divine insight that a corrupt spirit has enthralled the recesses of our Club's purview. Elder Borzomati was the first to write of this, bestowing upon them the diminutive '"s"-words.' Upon further investigation, the Sextet has qualified Elder Borzomati's identification and our Lord's divine premonition. Henceforth, all enemies to the Club will be referred to as 'the Sprinters' or 'the Serpents' at times in which discretion is needed. One should act amicably with Sprinters for they are not an intrinsically evil force but are rather corrupted and fallen. It is our duty to save but Clubmen are not allowed to fraternize with them throughout the course of our after-school practice hours.

Article of Conquest

On this date, the Twenty-Seventh of February, the Club engaged in a drawn-out battle against the Sprinters. It brings us great joy to say that the Club has been so blessed as to have been able to vanquish those heathens! However, it is prophesied that this battle will not be our final confrontation and on that matter all Clubmen should be ready to enter into battle upon First Call from any Elder. Furthermore, our Holy Land, the Recreational, must be defended at all costs for it is a sanctuary to our slow-twitch arsenal. That is, if any transgression happens, a Clubman must act upon it and halt it immediately, even if no Elder is present.

Article of Zones

All runs must stay in the second heart rate zone of cardiovascular activity. If one exceeds this zone, they must be working out or racing.

Article of Foods

Clubbers must consume Gu's or Maurtens on a daily basis.

This concludes our 'First Treatise.' Expect additions in the future.

— The Sextet, February 27, 2025

Declarations February 2025 3 min read

On the Matter of Our Labor

A call to arms in defense of our training grounds and our honor.

In accordance with the recent weather conditions, I believe that each and every one of you should be remembering the events which transpired a few weeks ago. If you for some reason do not remember, we were brutally persecuted due to our perceived "sloth", that is, the sprinters (or at least one of their higher-ups) presented a complaint against us. This complaint was not of a direct nature but was rather levied in a clandestine fashion to our dear Coach.

If this does not stoke the flames of revenge under your buttocks, quite frankly, I do not know what will! As such, I implore all of you to be ready to put your strongest foot forward. Arm yourselves, men! Arm yourselves with shovels, with gloves, with hats, with whatever gear necessary. We shall march on our beautiful track once again and this time we shall be the ones who stake a claim to it.

Get ready to shovel as soon as it is appropriate lest the sprinters win again.

— An Elder of the Sextet, February 2025
